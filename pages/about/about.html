Sabores del Sur is a casual and intimate Spanish-inspired restaurant, specializing in tapas and family-style dishes designed to share.

We bring Spain's vibrant flavors and rich culinary to your table. Our menu features classic tapas, flavorful paellas, and appetizing deserts and drinks, inspired by Spain's diverse regions. Dedicated to exceptional service and authentic cuisine, Sabores del Sur offers an unforgettable taste of Spanish charm and culinary delights.






Our History

In the early 1960s, in the picturesque town of Ronda, nestled in the heart of Andalusia, a culinary dream was born. María García, a passionate home cook with a deep love for traditional Andalusian cuisine, began hosting intimate gatherings in her quaint home. Her authentic dishes, rich in flavor and history, quickly garnered the admiration of friends and family. Word of María's culinary prowess spread, and soon her home became a local hotspot for those seeking the true taste of southern Spain.

Today, Sabores del Sur stands as a testament to the García family's enduring legacy. María, now in her nineties, still graces the restaurant with her presence, sharing stories and recipes with patrons. Antonio and Carmen, alongside their own children, ensure that Sabores del Sur remains a beacon of Andalusian culture and cuisine. The restaurant's rich history, combined with its commitment to quality and tradition, makes every visit a memorable journey through the flavors of southern Spain.

Our Recipes

Paela Mixta

Over the years, the García family's Paella Mixta evolved, with each generation adding their own unique touch while preserving the essence of María's original recipe. Antonio's children, Carmen and Miguel, introduced the dish to an international audience, and it became celebrated not just in Spain, but around the world.

Tortilla Espanola

García family's Tortilla Española remains a beloved classic at Sabores del Sur. Visitors to the restaurant can savor the same flavors that have been enjoyed by generations of the García family. The dish continues to be a source of pride for the García family, symbolizing their commitment to preserving and celebrating the rich culinary heritage of Spain.
